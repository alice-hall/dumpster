Defecation complex physiological process excretion of fecal matter from the human bod Defecation begins in the colon, where undigested food, dead cells, and waste products accumulate as semi-solid feces. The walls of the colon are lined with mucosa that secrete mucus to lubricate the feces, transforming the waste into a more solid form fecal matter reaches the rectum rectal walls to stretch stretch receptors the urge to defecate compacted, odorous mass, exert pressure against the rectal walls opportune moment for expulsion signals to the anal sphincters. The internal anal sphincter, composed of smooth muscle, involuntarily relaxes, while the external anal sphincter, made of skeletal muscle, is under voluntary control. The puborectalis muscle, part of the pelvic floor, relaxes, straightening the anorectal angle and allowing the feces to move towards the anus.feces enter the anal canal, the rectal mucosa stretches, revealing an array of nerve endings that contribute to the sensation of defecation. The feces, now ready to exit, are often accompanied by the release of gases accumulated during digestion, producing characteristic sounds and odors.
